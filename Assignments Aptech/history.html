The history of esports can be traced back to the early days of video gaming and computer technology. ​

  1. 1970s - 1980s: The birth of competitive gaming can be linked to the arcade era. Games like "Space Invaders" and "Pac-Man" initiated informal competitive gaming.​
  2. 1980s - 1990s: The rise of home gaming consoles and personal computers led to the formation of early esports communities. Titles like "Street Fighter" and "StarCraft" gained popularity, with competitive events taking place at local gatherings and small-scale tournaments.​
  3. 1990s - 2000s: The late 1990s saw the emergence of professional gaming leagues in South Korea, particularly in games like "StarCraft." South Korea became a hub for esports.​
  4. 2000s - 2010s: The 2000s marked a turning point for esports. Games like "Counter-Strike," "Warcraft III," and "Defense of the Ancients" (DotA) gained prominence. Esports organizations, sponsorships, and dedicated esports stadiums started to emerge. The World Cyber Games (WCG) and Electronic Sports World Cup (ESWC) were among the notable international esports events.​
  5. 2010s - Present: Esports continued to expand globally, with titles like "League of Legends," "Dota 2," "Overwatch," and "Fortnite" becoming major esports franchises. Prize pools for tournaments, such as "The International" for Dota 2, reached tens of millions of dollars​
  6. 2020s: Esports became more mainstream, with even traditional sports teams and celebrities investing in esports franchises. After pandemic there was an acceleration towards online competitions and livestreamed events, as physical gatherings were restricted.
